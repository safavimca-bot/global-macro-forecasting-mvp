# Methodology

Global Macro Outlook AI uses transparent rule-based scoring for the MVP. Each category is scored from 0 to 100, where higher means higher macro risk or monitoring priority.

## Score Categories

- Growth momentum: penalizes slowing GDP growth, below-trend growth, and rising unemployment.
- Inflation pressure: rises when CPI is above target, wage growth is elevated, oil rises, or FX depreciates.
- Monetary tightness: rises when real policy rates are positive, policy rates are high, or the yield curve is inverted.
- Fiscal stress: rises with high debt-to-GDP, wide deficits, and adverse rate-growth arithmetic.
- Credit stress: rises with weakening credit growth, high NPLs, and yield-curve inversion.
- External vulnerability: rises with current-account deficits, currency depreciation, falling reserves, and high external debt.
- Commodity exposure: links energy shocks to country importer/exporter profiles.
- Geopolitical and structural risk: a conservative placeholder score for non-cyclical risks.

## Regime Logic

The model classifies countries into expansion, slowdown, recession risk, stagflation, disinflationary growth, credit stress, external stress, or fiscal stress. High fiscal, external, or credit stress can override the baseline growth signal.

## Forecasting

The forecasting lab provides last-observation-carried-forward, moving-average, and linear-trend baselines. The confidence bands are illustrative and intentionally simple.
